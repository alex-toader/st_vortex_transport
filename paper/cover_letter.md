Dear Editors of Physical Review Letters,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript entitled "Phonon scattering by a disclination loop: forward decoherence and a flat Boltzmann transport integrand."

This work presents, to our knowledge, the first computation of the phonon transport cross-section of a disclination loop in a crystalline lattice. The central result is that the Boltzmann integrand is nearly constant across the Brillouin zone (CV = 5.6%), producing a transport coefficient of order unity without fine-tuning. We identify the mechanism responsible: an interplay of algebraic cancellation, vertex constancy at strong coupling, and forward decoherence through multiple scattering that shifts the collective exponent from $-5/2$ to $\approx -2$.

The result is supported by 192 automated tests mirroring the mechanism chain of the paper, with all source code and data publicly available. The flat integrand passes several nontrivial negative tests: it disappears without multiple scattering, at weak coupling, with next-nearest-neighbor gauging, and under the Aharonov--Bohm approximation.

This Letter should be of interest to the condensed matter community working on phonon transport and lattice defects, as well as to researchers studying scattering from extended defects in ordered media.
